About this model

This tool uses a Ridge Regression model trained on a dataset of 100 patients (19 real clinical cases + 81 realistic synthetics) to predict the Mean Articular Eminence Inclination (AEI) from routine radiographic measurements.

MAE: 3.15°
RMSE: 3.96°
R²: 0.33
n: 100 patients

An R² of 0.33 is consistent with published literature (Katsavrias 2002; Pullinger & Hollender 2002), which reports R²=0.15–0.35 for predicting eminence inclination from cephalometrics. Approximately 70% of AEI variance is explained by factors not captured in radiographic measurements (disc morphology, genetics, functional loading).

Top predictors: Ramus Height (+), ANB (+), Sex — Male (+), SN-GoGn (−). A higher AEI indicates a steeper eminence requiring a higher condylar guidance setting on the articulator.

For research and educational use only. Not validated for clinical decision-making.
